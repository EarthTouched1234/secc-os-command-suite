# 10-Minute Demo Script

*A click-through narrative. Property as the worked proof; PMO governance as the product.*
*Keep one rule: never call the data "live." It's a controlled proof-of-concept on real records.*

---

### 0:00 — Frame it (1 min)
> "I'm going to show you a management engine. We proved it on a property because property gives
> clean daily numbers — but what you're really looking at is a governance layer that runs any
> portfolio. Watch what the system does that a spreadsheet can't."

---

### 1:00 — The finished report (2 min) → `01_DAR-sample-report.md`
- Open the DAR. "This is The Eddy, real data, two weeks of June."
- Point to **95.8% occupancy, improving**, and the **work-order watch flag**.
- The hook: *"No one assembled this. No analyst typed a status. The system pulled, scored,
  flagged, and wrote it. The manager's job is now to act, not to assemble."*

---

### 3:00 — The governance (3 min) → `02_governed-promotion.md`
This is the differentiator — spend your time here.
- Walk the pipeline diagram top to bottom: **export → quarantine → validate → gate → promote.**
- "Every number is quarantined *first*. Production is never written directly."
- **Then show the rejection block.** Read it out loud:
  > "A bad record doesn't crash it and doesn't corrupt the report. It *refuses, explains why,
  > and logs it.* That's the difference between a tool and a governed system."
- Land the two rules: **enrich never mint**, **one frozen source of truth**.

---

### 6:00 — Scale it up (2.5 min) → `03_PMO-and-scale.md`
- Show The Eddy as **one program card** in the portfolio (RAG / health / trajectory).
- "The engine doesn't know it's property. It needs three things from any domain — a metric, a
  red line, a trend. Define those and it governs energy, construction, IT the same way."
- Show the vertical table. *"Property is the first program in your portfolio, not the last."*

---

### 8:30 — Close on economics (1 min)
> "Every PMO tool you've seen needs *more people* as you grow. This one absorbs the admin work
> and gives one executive full control. It scales down in cost as it scales up in scope.
> We start with property, on manual export, no integration risk — and automate the feed when
> you're ready."

---

### Anticipated questions

| They ask | You say |
|----------|---------|
| "Is this live?" | "This is a proof-of-concept on real seeded records. The connector that automates the feed is built and tested; it runs on manual export today, and a session token turns on automation — zero integration risk to start." |
| "What stops bad data getting in?" | *(Re-open the rejection block.)* "This. Quarantine plus a contract gate. Bad data is refused and logged, never promoted." |
| "Can it do *our* business, not property?" | "Yes — that's the point. Give me your primary metric, your red line, and your trend metric, and the same engine governs it. Property was just the cleanest place to prove it." |
| "Who can change things?" | "GUARDiAN. AI operates the machinery; a human holds exclusive authority over anything financial, structural, or security-related. Hard rule." |
